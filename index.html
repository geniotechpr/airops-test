How to Explain Search Changes to Your Team: A Guide for Marketers

▶️ Play Audio Narration

Search is not what it used to be. Ten years ago, marketers focused on keyword density, backlinks, and ranking on page one. Today, the search experience is different—and still changing.

AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's Search Generative Experience (SGE) now shape how people find information online. Instead of ten blue links, users receive AI-generated summaries, citations, or direct answers.

For marketers, the challenge is no longer just understanding how search works. It is explaining these shifts clearly to leadership, product, sales, and other teams that rely on marketing to drive visibility and growth.

The Evolving Search Landscape: Why Communication is Key

Search has moved from keyword-based systems to complex models prioritizing brand authority, structured data, and expert-backed content. Large Language Models (LLMs) now play a central role in how content is retrieved and presented. Tools like ChatGPT and SGE don't just list links—they summarize, explain, and sometimes bypass traditional websites altogether.

This shift introduces a communication gap. Stakeholders outside of SEO may not track these changes closely. They may still evaluate performance using outdated metrics or assume that old strategies apply.

Bridging the Communication Gap: Understanding Your Audience

Different teams have different priorities. Understanding these priorities helps you explain search changes in ways that matter to each group.

Speaking Leadership's Language

Executives care about business results, not technical SEO details. When explaining search changes to leadership, focus on:

  • Business metrics: Connect search performance to MQLs, SQLs, pipeline, revenue, and customer retention
  • Simple storytelling: Use a "What, So What, Now What" framework
  • Strategic alignment: Show how search initiatives support company goals and OKRs

Instead of saying "Google's algorithm update affected our rankings," try "The recent search changes reduced our visibility for key product terms by 30%, potentially impacting next quarter's lead generation by 15%. We recommend refreshing our core product pages with updated expert content."

Aligning with Product and Engineering

Technical teams work on different timelines and priorities. To get their support:

  • Avoid minimizing complexity: Never use "just" when requesting technical changes
  • Connect to their goals: Frame SEO needs in terms of site performance or user experience
  • Plan ahead: Get involved in roadmap discussions early

Use ai tools to translate SEO concepts into engineering language. For example, describe canonical tags as improving crawl efficiency rather than calling them an "SEO best practice."

Engaging Cross-Functional Teams

SEO challenges often connect to broader business issues. When explaining search changes:

  • Look beyond SEO metrics: Connect search performance to the full customer journey
  • Investigate root causes: Examine internal processes and competitor behavior
  • Build collaborative solutions: Involve multiple teams in addressing challenges

Practical Strategies for Effective Buy-in

Frame Technical Issues as Business Impacts

Technical SEO problems directly affect business results. When explaining issues:

  1. Quantify the impact ("This indexing issue blocks 40% of our product pages from search")
  2. Translate technical terms into user-focused language
  3. Connect problems to revenue or growth metrics

Master the Art of Storytelling

Structure your communication to make it compelling:

  • Use the "What, So What, Now What" framework
  • Show comparative examples: "Companies that updated their content saw 30% traffic increase"
  • Create clear hypotheses: "Implementing structured data will increase AI visibility by 40%"

Foster AI Literacy Across Teams

Simple approaches include:

  • Weekly prompting sessions: Practice using ai tools together
  • Internal idea boards: Create spaces for AI application suggestions
  • Practical demonstrations: Show real examples of traditional vs. AI-driven search results

Addressing Resource Limitations

When resources are tight:

  1. Prioritize high-impact changes affecting revenue-generating pages
  2. Connect search initiatives to existing business priorities
  3. Start small with proof-of-concept projects

Measuring Success in a Changing Data Landscape

The Challenge of Disappearing Data

Traditional SEO data is becoming less available. Keyword-level traffic information is limited, and AI-generated answers don't always drive website clicks. Today's approach requires combining data from multiple sources: search console data, website analytics, conversion tracking, and manual AI answer checks.

New Metrics for AI-Driven Visibility

In AI-powered search, visibility doesn't always mean clicks. New measurement approaches include:

  • Manual AI checks: Regularly test how your brand appears in ai tools
  • Audience behavior analysis: Use tools like SparkToro to understand ai tool usage
  • Integrated reporting: Track performance across all channels

Proving Business Impact Beyond Clicks

Connect search visibility to business outcomes by:

  1. Tracking content influence at each funnel stage
  2. Measuring conversion rates from organic traffic
  3. Calculating content ROI by comparing costs to revenue

Adapting Content and Strategy for AI-First Search

Creating Unique, Expert Content

To stand out from AI summaries:

  • Feature subject matter experts: Include unique specialist insights
  • Provide original research: Share proprietary data and case studies
  • Focus on specific niches: Create detailed content for specialized audiences

Using the FLIP Framework for Visibility

The FLIP framework identifies content likely to be cited by AI:

  • Freshness: Time-sensitive information
  • Local: Location-specific content
  • In-depth: Detailed, technical explanations
  • Personalization: Content tailored to specific user needs

Q&A

How do I explain AI's impact on search to non-technical stakeholders?

Focus on business outcomes rather than technical details. Show concrete examples of how AI-generated answers affect your visibility and quantify the impact on leads, traffic, and revenue. Use visual comparisons between traditional search results and AI responses to make the changes tangible.

What's the most important metric to track in AI-driven search?

While traditional metrics like rankings and traffic remain important, brand mentions in AI-generated content are becoming crucial. Track how often and how accurately ai tools reference your brand, products, and expertise. This visibility creates awareness even without direct clicks.

How quickly should we adapt our content strategy for AI search?

Start immediately if your audience uses ai tools or if you're seeing declining click-through rates despite stable rankings. Begin with high-value pages that drive revenue, then expand systematically. Industries with informational content (healthcare, finance, education) need to move faster than others.

What's the biggest mistake teams make when adapting to AI search?

Creating generic content that simply repeats common knowledge. ai tools already summarize this information effectively. Instead, focus on unique insights, expert perspectives, and proprietary data that AI cannot find elsewhere. This approach ensures your content remains valuable and citable.
